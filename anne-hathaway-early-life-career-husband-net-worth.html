Early Life and Education of Anne Hathaway

Anne Jacqueline Hathaway, aka Anne Hathaway in short and sweet, was born on November 12, 1982, in New York City, United States of America.

This American actress was the proud daughter of Gerald Hathaway, a labor attorney, and Kate McCauley Hathaway, a former actress.

Looking at her ethnicity, her father is of Irish, French, English, and German ancestry, whereas her mother is of Irish descent. Her first name, “Anne,” was kept after Shakespeare’s wife.

This beautiful actress of today was born after her elder brother Michael and before her younger brother Thomas.

Hathaway grew up in Short Hills, New Jersey, with her family. Her interest in becoming an actress grew after watching her mother performing on stage.

She went to Brooklyn Heights Montessori School then to Wyoming Elementary School, where she graduated.

For her higher studies, she went to study at the American Academy of Dramatic Arts and became the first teenager to join the Barrow Group Theater Company’s acting program.

Since her education journey, Anne began to work on movies and series, for which she had to leave her New York University.
