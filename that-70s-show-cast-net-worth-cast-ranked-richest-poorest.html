That '70s Show: The Cast Ranked From Richest To Poorest

That '70s Show aired from 1998 to 2006; it portrayed the lives of a group of teenagers residing in the fictional town of Point Place, Wisconsin, during the late 1970s. The show was known for its comedic take on the era, as well as its relatable characters and witty dialogue. Set against the backdrop of the disco era and the rise of punk rock, That '70s Show captured the essence of a generation coming of age in a time of cultural change and political turmoil, with some storylines too controversial to be repeated again.

The show's ensemble cast, including actors such as Topher Grace, Mila Kunis and Ashton Kutcher, brought their characters to life with their natural chemistry and comedic timing. From the basement hangouts to the iconic circle scenes, That '70s Show became a beloved sitcom that continues to resonate with audiences today. Netflix even launched a spin-off called That '90s Show, which has a second season planned for 2024.

Notably, it served as a significant platform for aspiring actors and actresses such as Ashton Kutcher and Mila Kunis, propelling their careers to new heights. What about the rest of the cast members, though? Where are they now, and how do they rank as far as net worth? Keep reading to find out!

9 Tommy Chong

Net Worth: $20 Million

  • Role: Leo

Tommy Chong played Leo in That '70s Show; his character was known for his laid-back and free-spirited personality. Leo was often seen as the resident stoner of the group, bringing a unique and comedic element to the show. Despite his frequent forgetfulness and occasional odd behavior, Leo's charm and humor made him a beloved character among fans of That '70s Show.

Tommy Chong had a big career prior to being on the show, as he was part of the iconic comedy duo Cheech and Chong. Their stoner-themed movies and stand-up routines gained a cult following, making Tommy Chong a recognizable figure in the entertainment industry, helping him earn a net worth of $20 million. His portrayal of Leo in That '70s Show allowed him to bring his comedic expertise to a new generation of viewers.

8 Wilmer Valderrama

Net Worth: $20 Million

  • Role: Fez

Wilmer Valderama has a net worth of $20 million (according to CelebrityNetWorth, he is worth $18 million). The actor has also continued his career since the disco era show ended. In fact, some parents may know him better as Handy Manny from the Disney Channel.

Valderrama has also gone on to appear in various television shows, like the popular series NCIS and From Dusk Till Dawn: The Series, further expanding his presence in the entertainment industry.

Perhaps most exciting for Valderrama is that he is in production on the film Zorro, in which he plays none other than the masked vigilante himself.

7 Topher Grace

Net Worth: $14 Million

  • Role: Eric Forman

Topher Grace played the son of Red and Kitty, Eric Forman. Today, he has a nice nest egg of $14 million and has been able to pursue a vast range of projects in both film and television. Following the conclusion of That '70s Show in 2006, Grace's career took off as he ventured into more challenging and diverse roles.

He displayed his dramatic prowess in films like In Good Company and Traffic, earning critical acclaim for his performances. Additionally, Grace showcased his comedic talents in movies such as Take Me Home Tonight and Win a Date with Tad Hamilton!

5 Kurtwood Smith

Net Worth: $10 Million

  • Role: Red Forman

Kurtwood Smith played the funniest father role in a long time, Red Forman. A role that helped him to earn a $10 million net worth. He was a classic example of a strict, no-nonsense dad who never failed to deliver hilarious one-liners. With his gruff voice and deadpan delivery, Smith brought Red Forman to life in a way that instantly endeared him to audiences. This is why Smith was brought back to play his role as Red in That '90s Show in 2023.

Whether he was berating his son Eric or his dim-witted neighbor Kelso, Smith's comedic timing and impeccable portrayal of Red made him an unforgettable character in the world of television sitcoms.

4 Christopher Masterson

Net Worth: $10 Million

  • Role: Todd

Christopher Masterson, the brother of Danny Masterson played the role of Todd in That '70s Show. He was a recurring character throughout the series, appearing in a total of 16 episodes. Since then, he has continued to act in various television shows and films, which helped him earn a net worth of $10 million.

Some of his notable roles include Francis in Malcolm in the Middle and Neil in American History X.

Masterson has not worked in the industry since 2019.

3 Danny Masterson

Net Worth: $8 Million

  • Role: Steven Hyde

Danny Masterson has appeared in various TV shows and movies since That '70s Show, including Men at Work and Netflix's The Ranch. He reunited with co-star Ashton Kutcher on the show from 2016 to 2020. This helped contribute to his net worth of $8 million.

In June 2020, he was charged with three counts of rape by force or fear, allegedly occurring between 2001 and 2003.

Masterson denied the allegations, and in December 2017, he was fired from The Ranch and his character was written out. His association with the Church of Scientology has also gained media attention in the legal proceedings.

Masterson is now serving a life sentence and his wife, Bijou Phillips has filed for divorce.

2 Debra Jo Rupp

Net Worth: $5 Million

  • Role: Kitty Forman

Debra Jo Rupp, better known as Kitty Forman played the sweet and a little loopy mom on That '70s Show. Her portrayal of Kitty Forman earned her widespread recognition and made her a popular character among fans of the show. It also helped her earn a net worth of $5 million.

Rupp's impeccable comedic delivery and endearing personality brought an undeniable charm to the role, making Kitty one of the most memorable TV moms of all time. A role that she reprised along with Smith in That '90s Show.

1 Allison Munn

Net Worth: $2 Million

  • Role: Caroline

Allison Munn delivered a remarkable performance as the charming and neurotic Caroline in the beloved television series. As Fez's romantic interest, her character added dimension and interest to the storyline. Even though her time on the show was relatively short, her presence left a lasting impression on both the characters and fans alike. It also helped her earn a net worth of $2 million.
