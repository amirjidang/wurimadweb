There is a common misconception that actors' lives are easy and that they make easy money, but even for the richest actors in the world, that's not always the case.

The world's biggest actors have memorized several thousand hours of dialogue; they've acted in trying situations, in front of thousands of people, amid distracting ambient chatter, or in deafening silence in solitude.

Actors have to learn specific movements to further mold their characters. After working challenging hours, meeting punishing deadlines, and working in dangerous locales, those actors were able to make their names as A-listers.

After that, their new struggle starts: they have to live every moment in the critical public eye, where every moment is noticed, and every aspect of their lives scrutinized.

Through depression, sadness, and difficult life situations, they have to maintain their image to maintain their careers.

Being a superstar is not an easy task, but some of them managed and became masters of their craft. They earned their gigantic paydays.

Well-known acting wizards like Tom Hanks, Sylvester Stallone, Mark Wahlberg, Jackie Chan, Jack Nicholson, and Brad Pitt are earning millions, and now they all have fortunes in excess of $400 million.

8 Mel Gibson

For his debut movie, Summer City, Mel Gibson earned just $400, but his "claim to fame" movie, Mad Max, changed his entire career, and he took home $15,000 for this life-changing role as Max.

Eventually landing huge blockbuster roles in movies like Braveheart and Lethal Weapon, Gibson later earned between $10 million and $30 million dollars for his roles in big films.

6 Arnold Schwarzenegger

Arnold Schwarzenegger’s Estimated Net Worth: $450 Million

However, his thick Austrian accent barred him from major roles. Nevertheless, Arnold didn’t lose hope.

After 14 years of struggling and waiting, he gained recognition with Conan The Barbarian, and a few months later, he landed a career and life-defining role in The Terminator.

Once a roadblock to success in Hollywood, his thick Austrian accent became his USP (unique selling point).
